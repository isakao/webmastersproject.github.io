The Costs

Solar power

Solar power presents an opportunity for homeowners to use clean, renewable energy while enjoying significant long-term savings.

For a standard residential solar panel system in the United States:

Small System (2-4 kW) - upfront cost may range from $6,000 to $12,000

Medium System (5-7 kW) - upfront cost may range from $10,000 to $20,000

Large System (8-10 kW) - upfront cost may range from $16,000 to $30,000

Investing in solar energy makes you eligible for the Solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) which provides 30% tax credit for photovoltaic installations. whitehouse.gov

Info about financial incentives and regulatory policies that apply to your home can be found here: dsireusa.org

Geothermal power

The Federal Residential Renewable Energy Tax Credit currently offers a tax credit of about 30% of the total cost of installing a geothermal heat pump system for homeowners. This plays a crucial role in making these systems more accessible and cost-effective.

On average, the upfront cost of installing a geothermal heat pump system typically ranges from $10,000 to $30,000 for a residential property.

While the upfront investment may seem steep, it's important to consider the long-term benefits.

Geothermal heat pumps are highly efficient and utilize the stable temperature of the Earth to provide heating in the winter and cooling in the summer. This results in lower operating costs compared to traditional HVAC systems. With proper maintenance, geothermal heat pumps can last for decades, further enhancing their value.

Additionally, homeowners may also qualify for state or local incentives, such as low-interest loans, further reducing the financial burden of installation. Beyond monetary savings, geothermal systems offer a more sustainable heating and cooling solution, reducing carbon emissions and lessening environmental impact. By leveraging available tax credits and incentives, homeowners can not only lower their energy bills but also contribute to a greener future while enjoying year-round comfort in their homes.

More information can be found on irs.gov
